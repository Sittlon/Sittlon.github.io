Privacy Policy for Poly Pew Pew

Last updated: 21 July 2026 · Version 1.0

This policy applies to the Poly Pew Pew Android and iOS app, its online beta, and these game, privacy and support pages. Apple and Google are separately responsible for processing relating to your store account, download and payment.

2. Summary

  • Offline play: You can play bot matches without an online account. Your local game progress generally remains on your device.
  • Online beta: When you use online features, an anonymous Firebase identity is created automatically and profile, lobby and match data is sent to Firebase.
  • Voluntary advertising: The app is designed to offer rewarded ads. Advertising and device information is processed subject to the privacy choices shown in the app.
  • Purchases: Apple or Google handles payments. The app processes technical purchase information, but not card or bank details.
  • Your choice: You can change advertising choices and request access, deletion and deletion of the anonymous online account.

3. Visiting this website

These static pages are delivered via GitHub Pages, a service of GitHub, Inc., 88 Colin P. Kelly Jr. Street, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA. When you visit a page, GitHub processes technically necessary connection and usage data, particularly IP address, date and time, requested URL, browser/device information and status and security data. GitHub determines retention according to the processing purpose and legal obligations.

We do not receive individual visitor profiles or raw server logs from GitHub. These pages use no first-party analytics or marketing services, external fonts or social media embeds and set no cookies of their own. An external link or email application opens only after your click.

More information: GitHub Pages – Data collection and the GitHub Privacy Statement.

4. Local progress and device storage

The app stores a local profile file containing, in particular, your chosen display name, coins and crystals, character appearance, map/mode choice, loadouts, weapons, skins, upgrades, attachments, match statistics, progress, goals, titles, reward status, and language, control, audio, graphics and display settings. In the current technical version, this entire profile file is not synchronised to Firebase.

Storage is necessary for the offline game you request (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR and, for necessary device access, section 25(2)(2) German TDDDG). Delete this data through the app's deletion function, the operating system's app-data controls, or by uninstalling. Depending on your device settings, Apple or Google may include data in a device backup. We cannot access or remotely erase your private iCloud or Google backups.

5. Online beta: Firebase Authentication and Realtime Database

Poly Pew Pew uses Firebase Authentication and Firebase Realtime Database from Google Ireland Limited, Gordon House, Barrow Street, Dublin 4, Ireland (“Google”) for online features. The Poly Pew Pew operator determines the purposes and content of game data; Google offers processing of many Firebase customer-data services under its data-processing terms. The concrete DPA/contract status must be confirmed before the public online release.

5.1 Anonymous authentication

When you first use an online feature, Firebase automatically creates an anonymous account with a stable user identifier (UID). A refresh token is stored locally. Firebase also processes authentication, IP, device, time and security information generated for technical reasons. The purposes are authentication, access control and abuse prevention. The requested online function is based on Article 6(1)(b) GDPR; necessary security measures are based on Article 6(1)(f).

5.2 Online profile, lobby and match

At least display name, character appearance and update time are stored with the UID as an online profile. Other players see your display name and appearance. Lobbies contain data such as UID, name, ready state, character, weapon, skin, map, mode and heartbeat timestamp. During a match, pseudonymous live game data is transmitted, including virtual position, view direction, movement, health, shots, hits, damage, critical state, attacker/victim UID, kills, deaths, team, match time and result. Virtual map coordinates are not physical location.

Processing is necessary for the online match you start (Article 6(1)(b) GDPR). Do not use a real name or sensitive information as your display name. There is currently no free-form chat and no photo, audio or video upload.

5.3 Location and deletion

The Realtime Database is configured in europe-west1 (Belgium). This does not mean all Firebase data remains in Europe: according to Google, Firebase Authentication runs in US data centres, while service and security data may be processed globally. Google identifies the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and Standard Contractual Clauses among its transfer mechanisms. More information: Privacy and Security in Firebase and Google Privacy Policy.

The online profile and anonymous authentication identity remain until you have the online account deleted. Lobby and live-match data is removed when a user leaves or a match ends normally. Immediate deletion following a crash or disconnection is not currently technically guaranteed; attributable remnants are removed as part of a verified account deletion. A fixed server-side maximum period and automated cleanup must still be implemented before the public online release.

6. Rewarded ads, UMP and ATT

The app is designed to offer only voluntary rewarded ads through Google AdMob. Google and the advertising partners actually listed in the privacy dialog may process consent status, IP address and an approximate region derived from it, device/app information, advertising identifiers where permitted, ad views and interactions, and diagnostic and anti-fraud signals.

In the EEA, United Kingdom and Switzerland, Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) is used before consent-dependent advertising is loaded. We rely on your consent for personalisation, tracking and non-essential device access (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR and section 25(1) TDDDG). Without the relevant consent, only restricted or non-personalised delivery modes permitted by your choices and the AdMob configuration may be used. Even non-personalised advertising can process data for delivery, frequency capping, measurement and fraud prevention. The core game remains available without personalised advertising or ATT permission.

On iOS, Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) is a separate system prompt for access to the IDFA and cross-app tracking. ATT does not replace GDPR/TDDDG consent, or vice versa. Before advertising is enabled, the app must provide an easy-to-find privacy option where you can reopen and change your choices. If it is absent from a test build, no consent-dependent advertising may be enabled in production; contact support in that case. iOS tracking can also be controlled under Privacy & Security → Tracking, and Android provides advertising-ID controls. A reward is granted for voluntarily watching a permitted ad, not for agreeing to tracking.

The recipient is Google Ireland Limited; depending on your choices, other ad-tech providers identified in the UMP dialog may be involved. Data may be processed outside the EEA. The providers and retention rules displayed in the dialog and described in Google's advertising information apply. SDK version, partner list and AdMob configuration must be reconciled with this policy before every release. No advertising is loaded on this website.

7. In-app purchases

Optional crystal packages are prepared as consumable purchases through Apple StoreKit or Google Play Billing. Apple or Google separately processes your store account, payment method, billing and payment data. Depending on the platform, the app receives technical purchase data such as product ID, purchase status, time, transaction or purchase token and store event to confirm, grant or restore a purchase. We do not receive complete card or bank data.

The legal basis is Article 6(1)(b) GDPR for purchase processing and crediting, and, where actually necessary, Article 6(1)(f) for fraud prevention and Article 6(1)(c) for records required by law. Separate server-side receipt validation is not currently active; this policy will be updated before it is added. Account deletion does not cancel or refund a store purchase. Request refunds through the relevant store.

8. Support and privacy requests

If you use an email link on the support or deletion page, your device opens your email application; the website itself does not transmit form data. If you send a message, we process sender address, name/pseudonym, content and correspondence, plus any platform, app version, UID/support code or necessary purchase reference you provide voluntarily. Messages are received in a WEB.DE mailbox operated by 1&1 Mail & Media GmbH; your own email provider also participates in delivery.

The purpose is handling your request. The legal basis is Article 6(1)(b) GDPR for game/contract assistance, otherwise Article 6(1)(f) (legitimate interest in support and troubleshooting), and Article 6(1)(c) for statutory data-subject requests. We delete support correspondence once the case is complete and no longer needed for follow-up, abuse prevention or legal claims. Evidence of rights requests is retained only as long and in as little detail as necessary to comply with and demonstrate statutory duties.

Do not send passwords, Firebase tokens, complete card/bank data, identity-document copies or particularly sensitive information unless specifically and lawfully requested. Attachments are not required. Mailbox provider information: WEB.DE Privacy.

10. Your rights

Subject to statutory conditions, you have rights of access (Article 15 GDPR), rectification (Article 16), erasure (Article 17), restriction (Article 18) and data portability (Article 20). You may withdraw consent at any time for the future (Article 7(3)).

Right to object: You may object at any time, on grounds relating to your particular situation, to processing based on legitimate interests (Article 21 GDPR). We will then stop processing unless compelling legitimate grounds or legal claims require otherwise. We do not send direct-marketing email.

Requests are generally free and answered without undue delay, normally within one month. We may request information needed for secure identity verification but do not routinely ask for more data than necessary. Email robertsittig1996@web.de or use the account deletion instructions.

You can also lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority (Article 77 GDPR). The authority responsible for our establishment is the Data Protection Commissioner of Saxony-Anhalt.

11. Children and young people

The app currently does not ask for age. Its target audience, store age settings and UMP/AdMob signals therefore must not be configured independently. Before a public release with online features or advertising, a binding decision will be made on whether children are included in the audience. If children are included, child-friendly information, parental consent where required, appropriate age assurance and child-appropriate advertising settings are necessary. If we learn that a child's data was processed without required consent, a guardian may request deletion using the contact details above.

12. Security and automated decisions

The website is delivered over HTTPS and loads no active third-party content. Online game data uses HTTPS connections, pseudonymous UIDs and Firebase access rules. Access rules and deletion processes must be tested before every release. Absolute security cannot be guaranteed. Secrets, passwords and admin keys must not be placed in the app or support messages.

Match rules, scores and automatic rewards currently do not produce decisions with legal or similarly significant effects under Article 22 GDPR. If Poly Pew Pew later introduces significant automated bans, profiling or personalised pricing, this will be reassessed in advance.

13. Changes

We update this policy before enabling new purposes, SDKs, backend services or data categories. We will ask again before consent-dependent new processing begins. The current version remains available at this URL; its date is shown at the top.
